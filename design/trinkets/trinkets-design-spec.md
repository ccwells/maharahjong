# Maharahjong Trinkets & Accessories Design Spec

## Overview
Small brass game tokens and markers that complete the premium Maharahjong set. All trinkets are solid brass with antique patina finish — the same material, scale, and craftsmanship as the dice. Each piece should be roughly dice-sized (~14–18mm) or slightly smaller, comfortable to hold and place on the mat.

## Material & Finish (shared across all trinkets)
- **Material**: Solid brass
- **Finish**: Antique brass patina (matching the dice exactly)
- **Scale**: ~14–18mm per piece — small enough to sit on the mat without interfering with tiles
- **Detail**: Recessed engravings with enamel fill in the Maharahjong palette (teal, magenta-pink, purple)

## Wind Marker Set (4 pieces)
- **Purpose**: Each player places one at their seat to mark wind position
- **Shape**: Square token with rounded corners, ~16mm × 16mm × 4mm
- **Design**: Each face engraved with one wind direction symbol and Devanagari character:
  - East/पू, South/द, West/प, North/उ
- **Fill**: Teal enamel in the engraved character
- **Reverse**: Maharahjong lotus-peacock logo, recessed

## Dealer Token (1 piece)
- **Purpose**: Indicates the current dealer (East wind player); passed each round
- **Shape**: Circular disc, ~18mm diameter × 4mm thick — slightly larger than the wind markers to distinguish it
- **Design**: Peacock-feather eye motif on the front, "East/पू" on the reverse
- **Fill**: Teal enamel eye-spot, gold surround

## Scoring Coins (3 denominations)
- **Purpose**: Track points during play; also serve as the "kitty" pot tokens referenced in the rules
- **Shape**: Coin-style, rounded edges
- **Denominations** differentiated by size and enamel color:
  - **1 point** (~14mm): Teal enamel center
  - **5 points** (~16mm): Magenta-pink enamel center
  - **10 points** (~18mm): Royal purple enamel center
- **Design**: Lotus-peacock logo on one side; point value in a decorative cartouche on the reverse
- **Quantity**: 80 × 1pt, 40 × 5pt, 20 × 10pt (140 coins per set)

## Ready Declaration Token (1 piece)
- **Purpose**: Placed face-up when a player declares "Ready" (Riichi-style, per the rules) to lock their hand
- **Shape**: Diamond/rhombus, ~16mm across × 4mm thick
- **Design**: Sri Yantra motif (echoing Vedic Symbols suit tile #9) on front; "Ready" in Devanagari (तैयार) on reverse
- **Fill**: Gold on brass (minimal enamel — the diamond shape alone distinguishes it)

## Production Notes
- All brass pieces should share the same antique patina treatment as the dice for visual cohesion
- Enamel fill colors should use the same Pantone references as the dice pips
- Scoring chips should be heavy enough to feel satisfying but stackable without toppling
- The dealer marker peacock figurine is the most complex piece — source from a brass casting or lost-wax specialist
- All fabric pieces (bags, felt pads) should use the same deep navy/teal as the mat background
