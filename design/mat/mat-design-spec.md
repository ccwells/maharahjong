# Maharahjong Playing Mat Design Spec

## Physical Specifications
- **Size**: 32.5 × 32.5 inches (square)
- **Material**: Machine-washable neoprene with non-slip rubber backing
- **Edge finish**: Contrast stitching around perimeter
- **Thickness**: ~3mm (standard neoprene gaming mat)
- **Print**: Full-bleed sublimation dye print on fabric surface

## Art Direction

### Primary reference
The gold-on-navy peacock mahjong playmat in the moodboard (`design/moodboard/nymmb.jpg`) is the closest analog and strongest starting point. Key elements to carry forward:
- Deep navy/teal field as the dominant background color
- Gold line art for all decorative elements (borders, motifs, center design)
- One peacock per corner, oriented toward center — each facing a different player seat
- Geometric art-deco framing lines connecting the corners
- Center medallion with a repeating pattern (the moodboard uses a peacock-feather fan pattern)

### Maharahjong adaptations
Differentiate from the reference by incorporating the Maharahjong visual identity:

**Corners (4 player positions)**
- Each corner features one of the four wind direction symbols from the honor tiles (East/पू, South/द, West/प, North/उ) rendered in gold, replacing generic corner art
- A peacock silhouette sits beside or behind each wind symbol, maintaining the moodboard's peacock-per-corner layout
- Corner art-deco framing uses fan/arch motifs drawn from Hoysala temple architecture rather than Western deco

**Border**
- Outer border: a continuous rangoli-dot band running the full perimeter, inside the contrast stitching
- Inner border: geometric line frame with diamond accents at midpoints of each side (referencing the moodboard's framing lines)

**Center medallion**
- A majestic Indian elephant in gold line art, facing forward, decorated with ceremonial regalia (howdah, jeweled headpiece, draped textiles, henna-style body patterns) — inspired by Rajasthani and Mughal elephant paintings
- Centered within a rotated-square (diamond) frame
- Surrounded by a rangoli mandala ring — concentric circles of lotus petals and peacock-feather eyes in gold line art
- The diamond frame sits inside a subtle, larger square outline that defines the wall-building area during gameplay

**Field**
- Deep navy/teal flat color — keep the playing surface clean and uncluttered so tiles are easy to read
- Optional: an extremely subtle tone-on-tone repeat pattern of tiny rangoli dots (visible only up close, not distracting during play). Reference the Indian textile repeat patterns in `design/moodboard/sUot9.jpg` for density and scale

### Color palette
- **Background**: Deep navy/teal (#1a2744 range — match the tile back design)
- **Line art & motifs**: Saffron gold (#c5a044 range)
- **Contrast stitching**: Gold or saffron thread to complement the print
- **Accent (optional)**: A single accent — magenta-pink or emerald green — used sparingly in the center medallion only, to echo the suit colors from the tile set

## Production Notes
- Design artwork at **6000 × 6000 px minimum** (185 DPI at 32.5 inches)
- Export as high-res PNG or TIFF for sublimation printing
- Verify gold tones survive the CMYK conversion for neoprene sublimation (metallic gold won't print — use a warm saffron-gold that reads as gold on dark backgrounds)
- Contrast stitching color should be specified as a Pantone or thread color code for the manufacturer
