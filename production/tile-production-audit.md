# Tile Production Compatibility Audit

**Date**: 2026-06-07
**Scope**: All 51 unique tile designs vs. eufyMake E1 (UV printer) and xTool F2 Ultra UV (laser engraver)
**Tile physical size**: 32 × 23 mm face

---

## 1. UV Printer Compatibility (eufyMake E1)

### Resolution check
- **Current images**: 1776 × 2368 px at 3:4 aspect ratio
- **Tile face**: 32 × 23 mm
- **Effective DPI at tile size**: 1776 px ÷ 32 mm × 25.4 mm/in = **1,409 DPI** (width) / 2368 px ÷ 23 mm × 25.4 mm/in = **2,614 DPI** (height)
- **Printer max**: 1,440 DPI
- **Verdict**: ✅ Width is within 2% of printer max. Height exceeds it. Images have sufficient resolution — no upscaling needed.

### Aspect ratio check
- **Current images**: 3:4 (1776:2368 = 1:1.333)
- **Tile face**: 32:23 = 1:1.391
- **Mismatch**: ~4.3% — the images are slightly narrower than the tile face
- **Impact**: Minor cropping or stretching needed. Not enough to distort designs.
- **Action needed**: ⚠️ Re-export at 32:23 aspect ratio (or crop to fit). Update `generate_tiles.py` to use a custom aspect ratio closer to the tile face, or post-process the PNGs.

### Color space check
- **Current images**: RGB (generated by Grok Imagine API)
- **Printer ink**: CMYKWG (6-channel UV ink)
- **Impact**: RGB→CMYK conversion will shift some colors. The most affected:
  - **Magenta-pink** (Lotus suit): Should translate well — magenta is a CMYK primary
  - **Teal/blue-green** (Peacock suit): May lose vibrancy — teal is notoriously hard in CMYK
  - **Royal purple** (Vedic suit): May shift slightly toward blue
  - **Saffron gold** (Wind honors): Should be fine — yellow + small magenta
  - **Gold metallic effect**: Cannot be reproduced in CMYK — will appear as a warm yellow-brown. The gloss layer can add sheen but not metallic reflectance
- **Action needed**: ⚠️ Print color test strips on tile material. Consider adjusting the digital palette to account for CMYK shift — boost teal saturation, check purple balance.

### File format check
- **Current files**: PNG (correct format)
- **Printer accepts**: JPEG, PNG, PDF
- **Verdict**: ✅ Compatible. PNG preserves quality for the level of detail in these designs.

### Background check
- **Current images**: Solid ivory/cream backgrounds (not transparent)
- **Printer needs**: Transparent backgrounds preferred for printing onto pre-colored tile blanks
- **Impact**: If tile blanks are already ivory/cream, solid backgrounds are fine. If blanks are white or another color, the printed ivory background may not match.
- **Action needed**: ⚠️ Re-export with transparent backgrounds as an option. Keep the current versions as reference.

### Batch layout check
- **Flatbed size**: 330 × 420 mm
- **Tile size**: 32 × 23 mm
- **Tiles per row**: 330 ÷ (32 + 2mm gap) = 9.7 → **9 tiles/row**
- **Tiles per column**: 420 ÷ (23 + 2mm gap) = 16.8 → **16 tiles/col**
- **Total per batch**: 9 × 16 = **144 tiles/batch**
- **Sets per batch**: 144 ÷ 152 = almost 1 full set (need a 2nd short run of 8 tiles)
- **Verdict**: ✅ Practical. Nearly a full set in one run.

### 3D texture opportunity
- **Capability**: Up to 5mm embossed height via layered white ink
- **Candidates**: Honor tiles (thicker gold border) and Rangoli bonus tiles could benefit from tactile embossing
- **Action needed**: 📋 Design which tiles get embossing; create depth maps for those tiles

## 2. Laser Engraver Compatibility (xTool F2 Ultra UV)

### Vector conversion check
- **Current files**: Raster PNG (AI-generated)
- **Engraver needs**: SVG vector paths for sharpest results
- **Impact**: AI-generated images contain complex gradients, shading, and photorealistic detail that cannot be directly vectorized into clean SVG paths. Auto-trace tools (Inkscape, Adobe Illustrator) will produce noisy, bloated SVGs.
- **Action needed**: 🔴 **Major work required.** Two options:
  1. **Manual vector redraw**: Trace each of the 51 unique designs as clean SVG line art. This is the highest quality approach but the most time-intensive.
  2. **Simplified engraving layer**: Create a separate SVG "engraving overlay" for each tile — just the gold borders, numeral cartouche, and key line details — rather than trying to vectorize the full artwork. The detailed color art comes from the UV printer; the engraver adds recessed structural details on top.

### Detail size check
- **Laser spot size**: 0.02 mm (20 μm)
- **Smallest design detail**: Devanagari characters in wind tiles (पू, द, प, उ) and Sanskrit seed syllables in chakra tiles (लं, वं, etc.)
- **Estimated character height on tile**: ~3–5 mm for wind tiles, ~2–3 mm for chakra syllables
- **Minimum stroke width at 3mm character height**: ~0.3 mm (well above 0.02mm spot size)
- **Verdict**: ✅ The laser can handle all text and fine details at tile size. Even the finest rangoli dot patterns (estimated ~0.5mm dots) are 25× larger than the spot size.

### Material compatibility
- **Tile material**: TBD (resin, acrylic, or melamine — not yet decided)
- **Engraver supports**: Acrylic ✅, plastic ✅, ceramic ✅, resin ✅
- **UV cold processing**: No burning/charring on any of these materials
- **Action needed**: ⚠️ Decide on tile blank material, then run test engravings

### Batch layout check
- **Work area**: 200 × 200 mm
- **Tile size**: 32 × 23 mm
- **Tiles per row**: 200 ÷ (32 + 2mm gap) = 5.9 → **5 tiles/row**
- **Tiles per column**: 200 ÷ (23 + 2mm gap) = 8 → **8 tiles/col**
- **Total per batch**: 5 × 8 = **40 tiles/batch**
- **Batches for full set**: 152 ÷ 40 = **4 batches**
- **Verdict**: ✅ Manageable. ~4 batches per set with L-shaped positioning piece for alignment.

### Print-then-engrave workflow risk
- **Risk**: UV laser engraving on top of cured UV ink could damage or discolor the printed layer
- **Mitigation**: Apply gloss overcoat after printing, before engraving. The gloss layer may protect the underlying print from laser ablation. Alternatively, engrave first (into bare tile), then print over the engraved surface.
- **Action needed**: 🔴 **Must test both workflows on sample tiles before production:**
  1. Print → Gloss → Engrave
  2. Engrave → Print → Gloss
  3. Engrave only (no print — gold-on-ivory aesthetic)
